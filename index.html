About Me

I moved to Los Angeles back in June of 2015 and it has been quite the journey. Originally from the East Coast, I graduated from NYU with a BSME in 2011. After college, I worked for General Dynamics Electric Boat as a Ship Systems Engineer II in the Habitability & Life Support Systems Group of the VIRGINIA Class (SSN) Program. Upon moving to LA, I transitioned from engineering and into Project Management.

In California, I have had the pleasure to work on a few exciting construction projects; USC The Village Project and LAX Terminal Redevelopment Project (T7 & T8). Today, I work at Lendlease Construction (US) as an Assistant Project Manager on the Oceanwide Plaza Project, a massive mixed use development, overseeing the Exterior LED Signage and Doors/Frames/Hardware trade packages.

With the completion of the UCLA Extension Coding Bootcamp, I aspire to make a change in my life and push myself to continue learning. I'm driven to take my career to the next steps and embrace the challenge of jumping into new and unknown territory.

Outside of work I enjoy rock climbing, skateboarding, hiking & backpacking.
