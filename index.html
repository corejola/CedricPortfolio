About Me

I moved to Los Angeles back in June of 2015 and it has been quite the journey. Originally from the East Coast, I graduated from NYU with a BSME in 2011. After college, I worked for General Dynamics Electric Boat as a Ship Systems Engineer II in the Habitability & Life Support Systems Group of the VIRGINIA Class (SSN) Program. Upon moving to LA, I transitioned from engineering into Construction Project Management.

In California, I have worked on several construction projects; USC: The Village Project and LAX Terminal Redevelopment Project (T7 & T8). Most recently, I was working at Lendlease Construction (US) as an Assistant Project Manager on the Oceanwide Plaza Project, a massive mixed use development, overseeing the massive exterior LED screens and Doors/Frames/Hardware trade packages. Unfortunately, due to financial issues on the project I was let go.

With the completion of the UCLA Extension Coding Bootcamp, I aspire to make a change in my life and push myself to continue learning. I'm driven to take my career to the next steps and embrace the challenge of jumping into new and unknown territory.

Outside of work I enjoy building model kits, running, rock climbing, skateboarding, hiking & backpacking.
